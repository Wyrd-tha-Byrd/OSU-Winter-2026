# Filippo Brunelleschi 
- Created the **linear perspective** technique in "modern" renaissance Europe
	- Leon Batista Alberti also helped in pioneering the technique
	- Would use a mirror and painting with sight holes to calculate the point of view sight lines
		- Used to match the painting to the subject with the sight lines
	- Vanishing points made painting more accurate to the realistic perspective

# Fresco
- Painting with intense pigments on the fresh plaster wall
- The techinque was commonly used in public spaces like churches but also found in private collections
	- Only private collections allowed to have nude representations of the body
	- Public collections featured revisions with leaves or clothes
